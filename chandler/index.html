The first step in effective termite control is a comprehensive inspection. Identifying Subterranean Termites

Subterranean termites are the most common type encountered in Arizona. They build distinctive mud tubes to travel between their underground colonies and their food source – your home's wooden structure. Our inspectors are experts at locating these tubes, even in hidden or hard-to-reach areas.

Identifying Drywood Termites

Drywood termites are less common than subterranean termites but can still cause significant damage. They live within the wood they infest and do not require contact with the soil. Signs of drywood termites include small piles of fecal pellets (frass) and damaged wood that appears hollowed out.

Targeted Treatment Options

Based on the findings of our inspection, we will recommend the most appropriate treatment plan for your specific situation. We offer a range of effective and environmentally responsible treatment options.

Liquid Termite Treatments

Liquid termiticides are commonly used to create a protective barrier around the perimeter of your property. This barrier prevents termites from entering your home and eliminates those that come into contact with the treated soil. We use advanced non-repellent termiticides that termites cannot detect, ensuring they unknowingly pass through the treated zones, effectively transferring the product back to the colony.

Termite Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems are an excellent alternative to traditional liquid treatments. These systems utilize bait stations strategically placed around your property containing a slow-acting termiticide. Termites consume the bait and carry it back to the colony, effectively eliminating the entire colony over time. Baiting systems are often a preferred option for properties with certain construction types or located near water sources.

Spot Treatments

For localized termite infestations, we may recommend targeted spot treatments. These treatments focus directly on the areas where termite activity is detected, providing a precise and efficient solution for minor issues.

Recognizing the Signs of Termite Infestation

Being able to identify the early signs of a termite infestation is crucial for minimizing damage and the cost of repairs. While some signs are more obvious, others are subtle and can be easily missed by the untrained eye. Knowing what to look for can empower you to act quickly and contact professionals like Tempe Termite at the first indication of a problem.

Mud Tubes on Exterior Walls and Foundation

Mud tubes, about the width of a pencil, are a definitive sign of subterranean termites. These tubes serve as insulated pathways, protecting termites from predators and the dry air as they travel from their underground colony to their food source within your home's wooden structure. If you see these tubes, even if they appear inactive, it indicates that termites are either currently or have previously been active on your property. Inspecting your foundation, crawl spaces, and exterior walls regularly can help you spot these early warnings.

Discarded Termite Wings

Termite swarmers, also known as alates, are reproductive termites that emerge from the colony to mate and establish new colonies. After swarming, they shed their wings. Finding small piles of shed wings near windows, doors, or light sources is a strong indicator of a nearby termite colony actively a part of the real problem you are having and they've been nesting and growing in size for a long time. The presence of wings, particularly indoors, suggests that a colony is either inside your home or very close by. These wings often have a translucent appearance and are uniform in size and shape.

Damaged Wood

Termites consume cellulose, the primary component of wood. Their feeding habits can cause significant structural damage over time. Wood infested by termites may sound hollow when tapped. You might also notice blistering or darkening of wood surfaces. In advanced infestations, you may even see visible tunneling or galleries within the wood. Termite damage often starts from the inside out, meaning the problem can be extensive before it becomes visually apparent. Inspecting less-used areas like attics, basements, and crawl spaces for signs of damage is particularly important.

Subtle Clicking Sounds Within Walls

While not always noticeable, some people report hearing faint clicking sounds coming from within their walls. This sound is thought to be made by worker termites as they feed on wood or by soldier termites banging their heads against the tunnel walls as a warning signal to the colony. While this sign is more subjective and may not be the first indicator you notice, combined with other signs, it can further confirm a termite presence.

Stiff or Difficult-to-Open Windows or Doors

As termites tunnel through wooden frames, they can cause the wood to warp and deform the shape of the frame, making windows and doors difficult to open or close properly. This symptom can sometimes be mistaken for general house settling or humidity-related issues, but it's important to consider termites as a possibility, especially if combined with other indicative signs. This is a sign that the structure of your home is being compromised.

Termite Droppings (Frass)

Drywood termites leave behind small, pellet-like droppings called frass. These droppings are often found in small piles near infested wood. The color of frass varies depending on the type of wood consumed. Finding these tiny pellets is a strong indicator of a drywood termite infestation and warrants immediate professional attention. They are often mistaken for sawdust or dirt, so a close inspection is necessary.

Presence of Live Termites

Seeing live termites is an obvious sign of an infestation. Swarming termites are typically winged and are often seen during warmer months, especially after rain and it is important to contact a professional immediately when you see active pests. Worker termites, which are creamy white and wingless, are usually hidden within their tunnels, but you might observe them if you disturb infested wood or break open a mud tube. Soldier termites, which have larger mandibles, are also a sign of an active colony. Recognizing the physical appearance of worker, soldier, and swarming termites can help you confirm an infestation and determine the type of termite present.

Addressing these signs promptly is critical. Delaying action allowing the infestation to continue and grow can lead to more extensive damage, costly repairs, and a longer treatment process. Long-Term Termite Protection and Peace of Mind

Beyond immediate treatment, Tempe Termite focuses on empowering homeowners with the knowledge and tools to maintain long-term termite protection. Understanding the environmental factors that attract termites and taking proactive steps to mitigate those risks is key to preventing future infestations. We provide valuable insights and recommendations tailored to your specific property and its surroundings.

Simple steps like ensuring proper drainage away from your foundation can significantly reduce moisture levels, making your property less attractive to subterranean termites. Repairing leaky pipes and faucets both indoors and outdoors is also crucial. Eliminating wood-to-soil contact by ensuring that wooden structures like decks, porches, or fences are not directly touching the ground removes a direct pathway for termites to access your home. Storing firewood away from your house and off the ground also helps. Additionally, regular inspections of your property, even after professional treatment, can help detect any new activity early before it escalates into a significant problem.
