Comprehensive Termite Control Services

Initial Termite Inspection and Assessment

The first step in effective termite control is a thorough inspection. Our technicians are trained to identify subtle signs of termite activity that might be easily missed by an untrained eye.

What our inspection includes:

  • Interior Inspection: We meticulously examine walls, floors, ceilings, attics, basements, and crawl spaces for signs of termite mud tubes, wood damage, and live termite activity.

  • Exterior Inspection: Our team inspects the foundation, exterior walls, wood structures, landscaping, and potential entry points around your property.

  • Detailed Reporting: Following the inspection, you'll receive a comprehensive report outlining our findings, the severity of the infestation, identified termite species (e.g., subterranean termites, drywood termites), and a proposed treatment plan.

Customized Termite Treatment Plans

Based on the inspection findings, we develop a tailored treatment plan. We understand that each property and infestation is unique, requiring a customized approach for maximum effectiveness.

Our treatment options may include:

  • Liquid Termiticides: Application of professional-grade liquid products around the foundation and entry points creates a protective barrier that eliminates termites upon contact and provides long-lasting residual control.

  • Termite Baiting Systems: Strategically placed bait stations attract subterranean termites, which then carry the toxic bait back to the colony, leading to colony eradication.

  • Wood Treatments: Specific treatments can be applied to susceptible wood areas to prevent termite infestations and eliminate existing ones within wood.

  • Fumigation: In severe cases of drywood termite infestations, fumigation may be recommended to eliminate termites throughout the entire structure.

Ongoing Monitoring and Prevention

Termite control is not just about eliminating the current infestation; it's also about preventing future ones. We offer ongoing monitoring services and provide recommendations to help make your property less appealing to termites.

Prevention strategies include:

Seasonal Pest Awareness and Termite Activity

Understanding termite behavior throughout the year in Scottsdale is crucial for effective control and prevention. Termite activity is influenced by weather patterns, particularly temperature and moisture levels. While termites can be active year-round in the favorable Arizona climate, certain seasons bring increased risks and noticeable signs.

Spring Termite Swarming Season

Spring, especially after rain showers, marks the peak of termite swarming season in Scottsdale. Swarming termites, also known as alates, are reproductive termites that leave their established colonies to mate and form new ones.

Why spring swarming is important:

  • Indicator of Infestation: Seeing swarming termites or finding their discarded wings (often near windowsills or light sources) is a strong sign that a mature termite colony is nearby, possibly within your property.

  • New Colony Formation: Successful mating pairs can initiate new colonies, increasing the overall termite pressure in the area.

During this time, it's essential to be vigilant and schedule an inspection if you notice swarming activity. Early detection is key to minimizing damage.

Summer Termite Activity

Summer heat can drive subterranean termites deeper into the soil in search of moisture, but established colonies remain active, diligently feeding on cellulose within your property's structure. Drywood termites continue their activity as well.

Summer concerns:

  • Continued Feeding: Termite feeding activity doesn't stop in the heat. Damage can progress unnoticed during the summer months.

  • Irrigation Issues: Over-irrigating lawns and gardens can create favorable conditions for subterranean termites near the foundation.

Maintaining proper drainage and addressing any leaks or moisture issues during the summer is vital for prevention.

Fall and Winter Termite Behavior

While termite activity may decrease slightly with cooler temperatures, it doesn't cease entirely in Scottsdale's mild climate. Termites remain active within insulated structures like homes and businesses.

Considerations for fall and winter:

  • Indoor Activity: Termites often seek refuge indoors during cooler periods, continuing to feed on wood within the structure.

  • Less Visible Signs: Swarming is less common, making it harder to detect new infestations without professional inspection.

Even during the cooler months, regular monitoring and proactive prevention remain important for protecting your property from termite damage.

Signs You Need Termite Control in Scottsdale, AZ

Being able to recognize the signs of a termite infestation early is crucial for limiting the damage. Termites are often called 'silent destroyers' because their activity can go unnoticed for a long time. However, there are several indicators that suggest you may have a termite problem and need professional termite control in Scottsdale.

Mud Tubes on Foundation or Walls

Subterranean termites build mud tubes, also known as shelter tubes, using soil, wood, and their saliva. These tubes serve as protected pathways from their underground colonies to their food source (the wood in your home). Finding these pencil-width tubes on your foundation, walls, or even indoor surfaces is a strong indication of subterranean termite presence. Disturbing these tubes and finding live termites confirms an active infestation that requires immediate attention from a termite exterminator in Scottsdale.

Why mud tubes matter:

  • Active Pathway: They are a lifeline for subterranean termites, allowing them to access wood without exposing themselves to the open air.

  • Indicates Location: The location of mud tubes can help pinpoint where the termites are entering your property and where their activity is concentrated.

Damaged or Hollow-Sounding Wood

Termites consume cellulose, the main component of wood, from the inside out. This means that the exterior of wooden structures may appear intact while the interior is being devoured. Tapping on wood and hearing a hollow sound, or discovering wood that crumbles easily when poked, are classic signs of termite damage. This damage often indicates a significant infestation that has been active for some time and requires expert intervention for termite removal in Scottsdale.

What hollow wood signifies:

  • Extensive Feeding: A hollow sound suggests that termites have consumed a substantial portion of the wood's interior.

  • Compromised Structure: Significant wood damage can compromise the structural integrity of your property, potentially leading to costly repairs.

Discarded Termite Wings

As mentioned during swarming season, reproductive termites shed their wings after finding a mate and establishing a new colony location. Finding piles of small, translucent wings near windowsills, doors, light fixtures, or in cobwebs is a common sign of a recent termite swarm and suggests that a termite colony has formed or attempted to form near that location. These discarded wings are a key indicator that you need to investigate further and likely require professional termite treatment in Scottsdale.

The meaning of discarded wings:

  • Swarming Activity: Indicates that a mature termite colony in the vicinity has produced swarmers.

  • New Colony Potential: While not all swarmers successfully establish new colonies, their presence means the potential for a new infestation exists.

Termite Droppings (Frass)

Drywood termites, unlike subterranean termites, do not require contact with the soil and tunnel within the wood itself. As they tunnel, they create kick-out holes to push out their fecal pellets, known as frass. These tiny, pellet-like droppings are often found in small piles near infested wood. The color of the frass can vary depending on the type of wood consumed. Finding frass is a strong indicator of a drywood termite infestation requiring specialized pest control in Scottsdale.

Identifying frass:

  • Appearance: Tiny, often hexagonally shaped pellets that resemble sawdust or sand.

  • Location: Typically found in small piles directly below infested wood.

Buckling Paint or Sagging Drywall

Termite activity, especially subterranean termites, can introduce moisture into walls as they tunnel and feed. This excess moisture can cause paint to buckle, blister, or peel, and drywall to sag or show stains. These signs can sometimes be mistaken for water damage, but a closer inspection by a qualified termite inspector can determine if termites are the cause. Addressing these signs promptly with termite control in Scottsdale can prevent further structural damage.

Why this happens:

  • Moisture Introduction: Termites bring moisture into structures, affecting materials like paint and drywall.

  • Underlying Damage: These visible signs often indicate significant feeding activity occurring behind the surface.

Clicking Sounds Coming from Walls

While less common for homeowners to hear, soldier termites sometimes make a clicking sound by banging their heads against the wood or shaking their bodies when the colony is disturbed. Hearing faint clicking or tapping sounds from within your walls could be an indication of an active termite presence. This is a sign that requires immediate investigation by a professional termite exterminator.

What the sounds mean:

  • Disturbance Response: The sounds are a defense mechanism by soldier termites when the colony is threatened.

  • Active Infestation: Hearing these sounds confirms the presence of live termites within the structure.

If you notice any of these signs, don't delay. Contacting Tempe Termite for a professional inspection is the best course of action to confirm the presence of termites and develop an effective termite treatment plan in Scottsdale, AZ. Addressing the problem early can save you significant time, money, and stress in the long run.

What Happens During a Termite Treatment in Scottsdale?

Once an infestation is confirmed and a treatment plan is developed, our skilled technicians will begin the process of eliminating the termites from your property. The specific treatment methods used will depend on the type of termite, the severity of the infestation, and your property's unique characteristics.

Subterranean Termite Treatment

Subterranean termites are the most common type of termite found in Arizona and require a different approach than drywood termites because they live in underground colonies and forage for food above ground. Our treatments focus on creating a barrier and targeting the colony.

Subterranean treatment steps:

  • Trenching and Rodding: A trench is typically dug around the perimeter of the foundation, and termiticide is applied to the soil to create a continuous protective barrier. For areas where trenching is not feasible, we use rodding techniques to inject the termiticide deep into the soil.

  • Foundation Drilling: In cases where termites are entering through the foundation or slabs, small holes may be drilled into the concrete to inject termiticide directly into the termite galleries beneath the slab.

  • Interior Treatment: If termites are active inside the structure, targeted treatments may be applied to infested wood or wall voids.

  • Baiting Systems: Installation of termite bait stations around the property perimeter provides an alternative or supplementary treatment method. Termites consume the bait and carry it back to the colony, leading to a gradual decline and eventual elimination of the colony.

Drywood Termite Treatment

Drywood termites live entirely within the wood they infest and do not require contact with the soil. Their treatment methods focus on directly targeting the infested wood.

Drywood treatment options:

  • Spot Treatments: For localized drywood termite infestations, we can apply liquid or foam termiticides directly into the termite galleries by drilling small holes into the infested wood. These treatments are effective for eliminating termites in specific areas.

  • Tent Fumigation: In cases of widespread drywood termite infestation, tent fumigation may be necessary. This involves covering the entire structure with a tent and introducing a fumigant gas that penetrates all infested wood, eliminating termites throughout the property. This is a highly effective method for severe drywood problems but requires vacating the property for a period.

  • Heat Treatments: Another option for drywood termites is heat treatment, where the temperature of the infested area is raised to a level that is lethal to the termites. This method can be effective for localized infestations.

Our technicians will explain the recommended treatment plan in detail, outlining the process, timeline, and any necessary preparations you need to make before treatment begins. We use safe and effective products and follow all industry best practices to ensure the health and safety of your family and pets.
